How to read this

Revenue. Install revenue = installs × kW × $/W × 1,000. Battery revenue = installs × attach% × $/battery. Maintenance MRR = active customers × ARPU. Total monthly revenue is the sum.

Gross margin. Variable costs are taken against install + battery revenue (not maintenance, which is treated as high-margin recurring). Permitting is per-install $. Sales commission is on closed install + battery revenue. Maintenance flows through at ~85% to reflect dispatched truck-roll cost.

Cash runway. Effective cash = cash on hand minus working-capital tied up in receivables (monthly revenue × AR days ÷ 30). Net monthly burn = fixed costs − gross profit. If gross profit covers fixed costs, runway is shown as profitable. Otherwise runway = effective cash ÷ net burn.

Break-even. Installs/month required so that contribution margin per install covers monthly fixed costs, net of maintenance gross profit. Contribution margin per install includes attached battery economics at the case's attach rate.

ITC note. The 30% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit accrues to the homeowner, not the installer, so it is not modeled as revenue here — but it materially supports the $/W the installer can charge. A repeal or step-down would compress price (model the downside).

Benchmark sources (accessed 2026-05)

  1. Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Tracking the Sun, 2024 edition — residential installed-price distributions and medians (~$3.30/W median, 2023 data).
  2. SEIA / Wood Mackenzie, U.S. Solar Market Insight, Q1 and Q4 2024 reports — residential pricing, segment shipments, battery attach trends.
  3. U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), Electric Power Monthly, Table 5.6.A — residential retail electricity price (~16.5¢/kWh national avg, 2024), and recent ~4%/yr growth.
  4. IRS, Residential Clean Energy Credit (IRC §25D), as amended by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 — 30% credit through 2032, step-down 2033–2034.
  5. Wood Mackenzie / SEIA U.S. Energy Storage Monitor, 2024 — residential storage attach rate (~25% national; >50% in CA NEM 3.0 cohorts) and installed-cost ranges (~$1,000–$1,500/kWh).
  6. NREL, U.S. Solar Photovoltaic System and Energy Storage Cost Benchmark, 2023 — residential cost stack (modules, inverter, structural BOS, electrical BOS, labor, permitting & interconnection, sales & overhead).
